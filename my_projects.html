Một Miếng

Traditional Bakery

The design of "Một Miếng" is rustic and approachable, featuring natural tones and handcrafted details that honor Vietnamese folk culture.

Read more

Brand Story

Tucked away in a small corner of the street, where the cool shade of trees gently sways, is a traditional pastry shop named "Một Miếng". It is a place for those who wish to rediscover the flavors of childhood and love the simplicity and rustic charm of rural Vietnam.

The traditional pastries, such as bánh da lợn (pig's skin cake), bánh chuối (banana cake), bánh đúc lá dứa (pandan rice cake), are topped with rich, fragrant coconut milk, becoming the signature of "Một Miếng". Each piece of cake carries the essence of hometown flavors, filled with the love and care of grandmothers and mothers—true artisan bakers.

When you visit the shop, you’ll not only experience the unique, delicious flavors of traditional cakes but also feel the warm, welcoming atmosphere of the "Một Miếng" family. We always greet our customers like family, bringing a sense of warmth and familiarity with every visit.

With passion and dedication, "Một Miếng" strives to be a favorite destination for many, where each piece of cake is not just a snack but a way to spread the rich culture of Vietnam to the Gen Z.

Meaning of the Brand Name

Inspired by the proverb "Một miếng khi đói, bằng một gói khi no", "Một Miếng" symbolizes that even a small piece can fully satisfy customers, not just in taste but also through service quality. Its memorable name helps the brand make a strong first impression.

The design style of "Một Miếng"

The project embodies a traditional and natural aesthetic, combined with the use of authentic Vietnamese materials and a simple, approachable layout. The design elements include:

  • Colors like soft yellow, orange, and shades of green, complemented by a touch of brown, aligning with a retro style.
  • Typography that is simple yet refined, easy to read and visually appealing.
  • Patterns and imagery inspired by banana leaves, bánh da lợn (Vietnamese layered cake), and familiar rural Vietnamese scenes.
